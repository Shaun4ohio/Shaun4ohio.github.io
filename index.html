WHY AM I RUNNING?

Problem: The US government has never been about liberty and justice for all, but it can be. Our politics are increasingly controlled by rich people who donate to political parties. Our representatives do not represent the concerns of everyday people- especially people from the inner cities and Rural America.

Solution:  I will fight for realistic policies that will have an immediate, positive, effect on regular people. We will have to fight the people who have a vested interest in the status quo.

Proposals:

  • Freedom Dividend: Monthly, unconditional, cash payment for all citizens age 18 and older. To be funded by a 10% sequestration on all federal agencies’ budgets and from a Greenhouse Gas Tax (GHGT).
    • Income security will help those who are displaced by the dynamic, global, and increasingly automated economy.
    • Giving labor more bargaining power will increase wages, expand the things that count as work, and help more people find work they actually like.
    • Redistribute wealth from rich to poor and from those who pollute more to those who pollute less.
    • Wealthy people contribute disproportionately to climate change and will be less affected in general. Poor people will suffer the most despite contributing less.
  • Complete Free Trade: 20% annual phase out on ALL import tariffs over 5-year period. Reduce or eliminate import restrictions on things like prescription drugs.
    • The only people who benefit from consumers paying more for “made in America” are those who sell that product. Everyone else pays more and has less to spend on other things. Example: US Pharmaceutical Drug Makers.
    • American companies also pay tariffs and/or “made in America” premium, and they pass this cost on to the consumer. A few people benefit, most people lose.
    • Greenhouse Gas Tax will be assessed on foreign products and fuel required to ship goods across oceans/borders. This will effectively be a flat tariff on all imports that will benefit local business when there is marginal comparative advantage.
  • Medicaid Opt-in & Healthcare Deregulation: Provide a public option for health insurance to undermine insurance companies. Deregulate medical care services to lower costs in the healthcare market.
    • We already pay more for health insurance than every other advanced nation, to worse results.
    • Health insurance companies as we know them today run a billion dollar racket and provide no tangible service. I welcome their demise, but getting rid of them will not solve all our problems. Having the federal government do a complete takeover of insurance is also undesirable.
    • Government must make it easier for new companies to provide healthcare. Examples: legal protection for companies operating in good-faith, reform drug patent system, reform medical licensure, reduce restrictions on currently illegal/prescription-only drugs.
    • Again, a few wealthy people and established companies benefit from our healthcare system (Steve Chabot is one) while the majority of people lose.
  • Reclaim War Powers: The average citizen has a say in US military action via congress, but only of the elected members want to address executive military action.
    • Pressure members of congress to vote on the authorization of ALL military force; even after the fact and even if it is inconvenient to them.
    • Repeal 9/11 AUMF for the war on terror and make congress vote every 90 days to keep troops in a forgeign country or not. The American people deserve to know who is keeping wars going and why.
  • Reform Immigration Law: Allow for free migration of workers and their families, who will be eligible for Dividend after 5 years of paying income tax and will be eligible to vote (full citizenship) after 18 years of law-abiding residency.
    • No matter their birthplace, people are people and we need to treat them like people.
    • Immigrants produce value, bring diverse knowledge and skills, create new businesses, and increase tax revenue.
    • People who come here seeking to improve their own lot will grow the pie for everyone and should be welcomed.
    • Laws should be applied to people equally regardless of their citizenship.

Problem: Our laws are too often designed by powerful people to keep everyone else under control. Those with money and influence can essentially do whatever they want. They can easily pay the flat fines assessed for petty infractions. They can afford the best lawyers and privacy when they actually do something horrible.

Solution: Repeal laws that cannot be equally enforced on everyone. Whenever the state must choose who is going to be held accountable, they inevitably choose the poorest, most vulnerable people. We must also redirect funding towards the pursuit of justice so that a defendant’s personal wealth does not have an impact on their criminal defense.

Proposals:

  • End the War on Drugs: Decriminalize the possession of all recreational intoxicants.
    • Allowed licensed, non-profit, agencies to produce and sell addictive substances, undercutting street dealers.
    • This will reduce money being spent in black markets, reduce deaths, reduce access for minors, and create an environment where addicts can seek help.
    • Selling drugs outside of this regulated framework will still be prohibited.
  • Reform Bail System: In America no one should sit in jail for more than 24 hours without being convicted of a crime, or arraigned for a violent crime.
    • The first step in this process is to stop arresting people for victimless and petty crimes.
    • For most offenses, suspects can and should be released on their own reconnaissance to await trial. Cases for which this is not viable should be expedited.
    • While spending will increase in the short term, reducing incarcerated populations will decrease spending in the long run; this is an investment in justice for regular people.
  • Demilitarize and Rebrand the Police: Police in this country are out of control. They are not judges or juries; they are certainly not executioners. Many of them have no business being public servants. They are enforcers of an idiotic criminal “justice” system that must be uprooted. I question the motives of an individual who would want to enforce our laws as they stand today.
    • Police serve the wealthy, influential, and powerful people- protecting them from everyone else. They do not protect or serve the poor because our laws do not serve the poor. We need to clean up our laws that allow broken window policing, and then clean house at most PDs.
    • Violence begets violence. It is time for local, state, and federal governments to stop paying people to escalate violence. Any officer who escalates a situation is unfit to serve and must be blacklisted.
    • Most of the money spent on policing would be better spent on the front end to attack the causes of lawless behavior.
  • Abolish the Department of Homeland Security: DHS has outlived its usefulness, if it ever had any. It should come as no surprise that the agency created to protect us at any cost would end up violating our right to privacy turning its guns on citizens protesting an unjust system.
    • The concentration camps of non-citizens for the “crime” of being in America is abhorrent and must end. We should not want a government capable of rounding people up into camps.
    • You don’t have to look very far into the past to see American citizens have their right to freedom stripped away in a racist and counterproductive manner.
    • Using DHS budget money to create a strong financial safety net for people will have a better effect on public safety than anything the DHS does.

Problem: Human activity is changing the climate in chaotic ways. Right now we have carbon communism, where everyone is financially responsible for the cost of everyone else’s carbon emissions. As a result, individuals have little incentive to invest in the reduction of their own carbon footprint. Companies have little incentive to reduce their footprint because it usually adds to the cost of their product and makes them less competitive.

Solution: We must find a realistic way to reduce our greenhouse gas emissions as soon as possible. We must do so in a way that helps the people who will suffer most both from the changing climate and from the increased costs of a carbon-free economy. Such a policy should incentivize individuals and companies alike to reduce their carbon footprint by providing a financial reward to do so.

Proposals: 

  • Greenhouse Gas Tax (GHGT): Impose a tax on energy generation, fuels production, and other industrial processes to establish a price for greenhouse gas emissions. The cost should be based on an estimate of their contribution to the financial cost of climate change.
    • Redistribute tax income with the Freedom Dividend so that the regressive, inflationary effects of such a consumption tax are offset.
    • Income being equal, people/companies who are above-average polluters will lose money, while those who are below average polluter swill gain money
    • Taxing carbon emissions will incentivize companies to invest in reducing their energy use and eventually transition to zero net carbon energy like nuclear, bio-gas, wind, and solar.
    • Create a market for carbon offset programs like sequestration, composting, and capture, etc. by allowing for negative emissions to be assessed a negative tax.
  • National Lab Expansion: We currently have 17 national labs funded by the Department of Energy; I am calling for at least one national lab in every state.
    • These labs should have corporate sponsors collaborate on shared problems. Example: Boeing & GE sponsoring green jet fuel research.
    • These labs would have development programs for those seeking to enter skilled, non-STEM trades, as well as entry level STEM jobs for HS grads.
    • Discoveries, reports, and patents produced by these labs must be made free and accessible to everyone in the world, forever.
  • National Rapid Transit System: Domestic airline flights and personal vehicle commutes are a massive and unnecessary source of greenhouse gas pollution that need to be reduced as much as possible.
    • We need an investment in green infrastructure the likes of which we have not seen since the Highways Act.
    • This new project should try to undo the racist legacy of our existing car-centered infrastructure.
    • Department of Defense money should be redirected towards our infrastructure instead of building new warships, warplanes, missiles etc.
  • Licensure Support & Reform: Money should not be an obstacle for those seeking professional licensure; especially if they already have student debt.
    • If a license is required by the state to perform a job, that state must provide materials and tests to all qualified applicants free of charge.
    • There must be transparency in the licensure process. State boards should not be limiting the supply of professionals to ensure higher wages for those already with a professional license.
    • Generally, we must make education, licensure, and knowledge cheaper and more accessible to everyone. It is time to stop letting companies profit off information that should be provided as a public good.
  • New Nuclear in Ohio: Ohio is an excellent state to build nuclear reactors; lots of space and low probability of natural disasters. We need to build some in order to be 100% carbon free and help stabilize the electric grid.
    • Climate refugees are likely going to flock to states like Ohio as sea levels rise and hurricanes continue to obliterate communities; We need to prepare for increased residential and industrial electricity users.
    • I would like to see Ohio build at least one AP1000 and 10 Nucsale SMRs.
    • The funding should come from a consortium of public bonds, corporate investment, and power plant shares for individuals.

Election Math:

There are two important variables that will determine the success of my campaign: Non-Voter Turnout Rate (NVT), and Major Party Defection Rate (DR). The total estimated voting population in the district is 560,000, of which I consider 300,000 to be likely voters based on the previous two elections. This leaves 260,000 likely non-voters. The equations for my votes and the republican votes are as follows.

My Votes = 300,000 × DR + 260,000 × NVT

R Votes = 0.55 × 300,000 × (1 – DR)

The assumptions behind these equations are that Republicans make up 55% of the likely voters (R+5 District), and that they are unlikely to gain a significant share of non-voters. By plugging in values for the DR and NVT, I can figure out the magic numbers that will give me more votes than Steve Chabot. It turns out that if both are just 25% (0.25), I win the election by almost 20,000 votes. My strategy will be to convert 25% of Democrat voters, 25% of Republican voters, and to turnout 25% of the non-voting population.

We can do this!
We can win!

This seems daunting, especially because I don't plan on raising even $400,000 dollars, but it can work if we manage to maintain exponential growth until november. Long Term Plan:

Long term, I hope to become part of a non-party caucus in congress. When this group is large enough to prevent either democrats or republicans from forming a majority in the House, the two party reign is over. Right now, in addition to being able to pass votes, the majority side gets to pick the Speaker of the House, an incredibly powerful and underrated position. The speaker gets essentially full control over the legislative agenda and house committee chairs/members. The speaker is chosen by a majority role call vote, which currently has only two warring factions. If the non-party caucus has enough members to force the democrat and republican candidates for speaker to compromise and form a coalition, there will be no more partisan stranglehold in the House. This process is similar to how other countries choose their Prime Minister.
